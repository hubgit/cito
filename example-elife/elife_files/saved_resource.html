    Editors’ choice

    With super-resolution microscopy, one can observe structures that are much smaller than the wavelength of visible light. In this paper Grotjohann et al. report that a new reversibly photoswitchable protein can be used to achieve dramatic improvements in both the resolution and speed of a form of super-resolution imaging called RESOLFT.

    Detlef Weigel
    Deputy Editor, eLife
